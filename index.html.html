Cristiano Ronaldo is a Portuguese professional footballer widely considered one of the greatest players of all time. He was born on February 5, 1985, in Madeira, Portugal. Ronaldo has won numerous awards, including five Ballon d'Or titles, which are given to the world's best player.
Ronaldo's career took off at Sporting CP before he moved to Manchester United in 2003. At United, he won several domestic titles, including three Premier League titles. He then moved to Real Madrid in 2009 for a then-record transfer fee, where he became the club's all-time top scorer, winning four Champions League titles and several La Liga trophies.

After leaving Madrid in 2018, he played for Juventus for a few years, continuing to break records. He returned to Manchester United in 2021, but later moved to Al Nassr in Saudi Arabia in 2023.

Ronaldo is also a key player for the Portuguese national team, leading them to victory in the 2016 UEFA European Championship and the 2019 UEFA Nations League.

Known for his incredible physical attributes, skill, and goal-scoring ability, Ronaldo has had a remarkable impact on football globally.
